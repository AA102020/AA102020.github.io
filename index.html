Biography

Abdul Ahmed is a researcher and strategist working across responsible AI, institutional risk, computational science, banking, defense technology, technology policy, and the digital economy. His work focuses on how advanced computational methods, machine learning, data science, and risk modeling can be applied to complex problems in financial systems, governance, security, and public policy.

His research spans AI governance, algorithmic command, machine learning for security analytics, consumer finance, banking governance, economic knowledge production, institutional reform, social organization, GIS, floodplain mapping, water resources, and computational modeling. Across these areas, his central concern is how technical systems become usable for decision-making, policy, institutional review, and risk management.

He is affiliated with Informatics AI in Washington, DC, maintains a verified academic email domain through Virginia Tech, and uses ORCID as a persistent scholarly identifier.

Selected Works

Reliability of AI-Generated Analytic Deliverables

Reliability of AI-Generated Analytic Deliverables: A Stochastic Workflow Evaluation Procedure. A. Ahmed. Working manuscript, May 2026.

SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=6789343

Develops a stochastic workflow reliability evaluation procedure for AI-generated analytic deliverables, including repeated-run diagnostics, source-role traceability, workflow-choice discipline, task-conditioned human comparison, and decision-use thresholds.

Inspection Regimes and Institutional Standing

Decipimur Specie Recti - Inspection Regimes: How Generative AI Deliverables Become Valid and Admissible Work Products. A. Ahmed. Working paper, May 2026. DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20268016.

SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=6776300

Develops an STS account of how AI-generated deliverables become valid, reviewable, admissible, and institutionally usable through registration, containment, inspection, classification, authorization, controlled circulation, maintenance, and reinspection.

Algorithmic Command and Defense AI Governance

Algorithmic Command: Policy Dilemmas for the Age of AI Warfare. A. Ahmed.

Examines how AI-enabled military decision systems create policy dilemmas around accountability, opacity, meaningful human control, ethical judgment, autonomous decision-making, and governance at machine speed.
